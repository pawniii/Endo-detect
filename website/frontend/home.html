Endometriosis: Meaning and Impact

Endometriosis is a chronic condition in which tissue similar to the lining inside the uterus grows outside the uterus, leading to pain, inflammation, and sometimes fertility issues. It affects an estimated 10% of women worldwide, with significant health, emotional, and social repercussions. In India, approximately 1 in 10 women suffer from this condition. Globally, it is estimated that over 176 million women are affected by endometriosis, making it a major health concern.

Impact on Quality of Life: Endometriosis significantly affects women's quality of life, leading to chronic pain, emotional distress, and challenges in personal relationships. The condition can interfere with work, education, and daily activities, requiring comprehensive management strategies to address both physical and psychological aspects of the condition.

Challenges in Diagnosis

Symptom Overlap Issues

Endometriosis symptoms often mimic other conditions, complicating accurate diagnosis and prolonging patient suffering.

Invasive Diagnostic Procedures

Relying on laparoscopy can delay crucial treatment and expose patients to surgical risks that might be avoidable with less invasive methods.

Social Stigma and it's Effects

Cultural perceptions surrounding women's health may hinder open discussions, leading to underreporting of symptoms.
